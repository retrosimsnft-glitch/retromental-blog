🧠 G.R.O.W. : Getting Rid of Old Ways

October 09, 2025

A Mental Brain Food Guide to Outgrowing What No Longer Serves You

Growth Means Letting Go

A lot of people talk about growth like it’s just adding new habits or stacking more wins. But real growth?

It’s subtractive.

It’s realizing that what once kept you safe is now keeping you stuck.

It’s noticing patterns that don’t serve you anymore and having the courage to release them.

Growth isn’t always loud. Sometimes, it’s as quiet as walking away from an old version of you.

💬 Mental Brain Food:

“To evolve, you gotta be willing to unlearn.” -RetroMental

🔹 G – Getting Honest with Yourself

Before anything else, growth starts with honesty.

Not with other people, with you.

Ask yourself:

  • What am I still holding onto out of fear?
  • What beliefs are running my life that I didn’t choose?
  • Is this version of me really aligned with where I want to go?

Self-honesty isn’t easy, but it’s the key to transformation.

🔹 R – Releasing What’s Familiar but Unhealthy

Old ways often feel safe because they’re familiar, not because they’re right.

Let go of:

  • The need to people-please
  • That inner voice that only speaks in doubt
  • The habits you use to numb instead of heal
  • The version of yourself that shrinks to fit spaces you’ve outgrown

Letting go makes room for better.

🔹 O – Owning Your Choices

Growth doesn’t happen on accident, it takes ownership.

That means:

  • Taking accountability without self-blame
  • Forgiving yourself for not knowing better sooner
  • Choosing healing over hiding
  • Moving forward with intention

Every choice you make is either pulling you closer to your future or locking you to your past.

🔹 W – Walking in Your Next Level

Once you’ve started shedding the old, you don’t go back.

Now you walk like you’ve already become the person you’re working on.

Not perfect. Not finished.

But growing intentionally, unapologetically.

This isn’t about proving anything.

It’s about becoming.

💬 Mental Brain Food:

Growth doesn’t need permission. Just movement.

🔚 Final Thought: Growth Is a Cycle

You don’t GROW once. You grow again and again.

Each time, deeper. Each time, freer.

So next time you feel like you’re falling apart, maybe you’re just shedding what no longer fits.

Get rid of old ways. G.R.O.W.
